How I Work
    You provide a brief overview of your organization and the service(s) you require. Feel free to get in touch with me using the contact form! The initial outline doesn’t need to be in any great detail, just enough to give an idea of your challenge or what you need. We hold scoping conversations to outline the framework, format and scale of the project. We talk as often as needed to work out the details! I’m highly collaborative and will ask you a lot of questions in order to tease out precisely what you’re after. I produce a proposal, the details of which are agreed between both parties. My initial proposal will include a methodology, deadline, schedule and cost. These are not set in stone - we negotiate the final terms and ensure you get exactly what you need. I provide the final product within the agreed deadline and respond to any feedback. Upon receipt of deposit, I get to work, engaging with you throughout the process to keep us on the same page. I will also resolve any follow-up queries within the scope of the agreed project. I issue an invoice, with final payment to be made within one month of project completion. I appreciate the financial constraints that differ between organizations, and so provide a grace period for payments.

FAQs

What types of organizations do you work with?

I will work with any group with a philanthropic or social benefit mission. This can include (but is not limited to):

  • 501(c)(3) charities
  • Social enterprises
  • Foundations (operating and non-operating)
  • NGOs
  • Charitable trusts
  • Public sector organizations
  • Corporate philanthropic arms

I specialize in working with smaller organizations who lack the resources for dedicated research staffing, but welcome requests from institutions of all sizes.

Do I need a specific project or approach in mind?

Not at all! If you identify a need or problem, I will help clarify what that is and how you might address it. The solution may not be a service I can provide but I will work with you to advise on what your next steps could be.

Why do you offer pro bono work for new clients, and how much does that offer include?

I appreciate that all nonprofit organizations must carefully justify any expenditure on back office functions and operations. I also know that for new clients I am an unknown quantity. By working your first project pro bono, I get to demonstrate the quality of my services and the value I can provide your organization, and I contribute to a worthy cause. Everyone wins!

Usually my pro bono projects will be up to about 10 hours of work. Anything more than that I will flag early on and negotiate.

Do you work pro bono for anyone other than new clients?

Unfortunately not. Much as I want to help you in any way I can (I wouldn’t be doing this otherwise!), this is my livelihood and I have a powerful need to eat!

How do you determine the cost of a project?

As part of my project proposal, I will provide a fee based on the complexity of the research, the use of specialist resources and the time it will take. The time estimate will be based on previous comparable projects.

What geographic area do you serve?

I’m based in Massachusetts, so most of my clients are in New England, but if your organization can engage with me without in-person discussions I’m more than happy to help those further afield!

At this time, I am unable to accept work for organizations outside the United States.

How does the referral discount work?

If an organization engages my services and lets me know who suggested PPR, I will ensure that the next project I undertake with that referee includes a 15% discount added to the proposal.

Make sure that if you were referred you let me know who by so they can take advantage of the discount!

About Me

Originally from the UK, I moved from academic history to working for the British government as a policy researcher and legislative affairs expert. Trained to be deployed at short notice to provide expert analysis on any topic as needed, I provided policy recommendations and justification to senior government officials and Ministers. I produced high-level briefing on subjects as varied as Brexit, environmental protection legislation, Eurozone economics, diplomatic relations, public sector transparency law, international trade agreements, healthcare systems, renewable energy, anti-corruption measures and the protection of democratic rights.

When I moved to the US, I knew I wanted to use my experience to benefit society by helping the nonprofit sector. During my time as a volunteer I realized that many smaller philanthropic organizations would benefit substantially from some professional research, policy analysis and advice; however, they often lack the in-house skills or financial capacity to divert paid staff or volunteers.

To that end, I started working freelance to provide affordable research support to worthy causes.
